Mini Hydroelectric Power Generation Model 💧⚡

I built a working mini hydroelectric power generation model based on the same principle used in traditional water wheels and modern hydroelectric plants.

Water is stored at a higher level and allowed to fall due to gravity. As it flows, it strikes a turbine wheel, rotating it. This rotation drives a generator, converting mechanical energy into electrical energy.

Energy conversion: Gravitational potential → kinetic → mechanical → electrical.

This model demonstrates the generation stage of hydroelectric systems and reflects the idea used in pumped-storage plants, where water is reused by moving it back to a higher level during low-demand periods.

What we learned:

  • How water height affects power generation.
  • How moving water creates rotational motion.
  • How generators convert motion into electricity.
  • Basic efficiency and energy-loss concepts.
